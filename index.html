Final plan

911-DHH: Emergency Support Website for Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing People

A simple emergency website that helps users contact police, ambulance, or fire services without speaking.

Problem

Most 911 systems depend on voice calls. Deaf users can face delay, confusion, and dangerous misunderstanding.

Solution

Provide an accessible way to contact emergency support through text, live video, GPS location sharing, emergency contact alerts, and stored medical information.

Users

Deaf people, Hard-of-Hearing people, emergency officers, and family or emergency contacts.

Technology

Frontend with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, plus PHP or Node.js, MySQL, Geolocation API, real-time messaging, and WebRTC for video call support.

How it works

User opens the website, chooses register or immediate support, selects the emergency type, sends the request and location, then continues with text chat or live video.

Benefits

Faster help, clearer communication, safer response, accessibility, and less misunderstanding during emergencies.

Future Improvements

More languages, AI chatbot support, improved video quality, real SMS alerts, and connection with official 911 systems.
